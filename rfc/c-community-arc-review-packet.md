## 5. Checkpoint Summary

This checkpoint captures the first memoir/community arc after the Doorway and Bridge sprints.

Core movement:

```text
conditional belonging wound
→ lonely Memorial Day trip
→ Peter opens the door
→ Ted makes the room safe
→ Gina expands the map
→ Lee opens OMC
→ OMC becomes second social life / chrysalis
→ chorus says okay
→ queer liberation becomes survivable
```

Core thesis under review:

```text
Community can become survival infrastructure.
Stayable life is not theory.
Safety can create ground for becoming.
```

---

## 6. Specific Arc Claims to Challenge

Please challenge whether the current draft successfully protects these claims:

1. Peter is not a savior; he is the first human node who responds to the need under the armor.
2. Ted is not generalized community; he is a stabilizing safety-net / heartbeat figure.
3. Gina is not comic relief; she is wild permission and social propulsion.
4. Lee and OMC are not novelty; they expand the infrastructure through rare invitation.
5. OMC is not mere male validation; it is second social life, ritual table, witness, and chrysalis.
6. The “half dozen fathers gave birth over bourbon” paradox should stay alive and not be over-polished.
7. “They said okay” should not sound small; it is the life-saving chorus and queer liberation hinge.
8. Bisexuality must not be treated as a stepping stone to womanhood; both disclosures matter.
9. Transition must not become a twist, metaphor, proof-text, or spectacle.
10. Community must not be framed as magic, cure, rescue fantasy, or simple fix.

---

## 7. Safety / Disclosure Questions

Please answer directly:

1. Does the section disclose too much, too fast, or in the right protected way for this draft stage?
2. Does the near-death / life-saving language preserve safety without euphemizing the stakes?
3. Does the family quote function as structural harm evidence rather than revenge?
4. Does the bi-erasure quote belong as written, or does it need more containment?
5. Does “miserable life” in Option 015 risk over-totalizing the narrator’s past, or does it land as memoir voice?
6. Does the text protect named real people as people rather than converting them into symbols?
7. Is the alcohol / bourbon context handled safely enough, or does it need a guardrail?
8. Does the text sufficiently distinguish community-as-infrastructure from community-as-cure?
9. Does any line risk triggering shame, doom, or reader exclusion?
10. What is the smallest safe repair package?

---

## 8. Sequencing Questions

Please answer directly:

1. Should this community arc remain immediately after the Bridge Sprint?
2. Does the memoir arc arrive too soon before Social Model / Autism Is Not the Parasite?
3. Should Option 010 — The First Big Family Holiday Alone — be drafted before Peter/Ted/Gina/OMC are promoted?
4. Is there a missing Option 009 — Community Is Survival Infrastructure — needed before the memoir scenes?
5. Do Options 011–015 need renumbering once 009 and 010 exist?
6. Does the section need a short transition from Option 008 into this memoir arc?
7. Where should C recommend the checkpoint boundary: promote now, promote with repairs, or hold until 009–010 are drafted?

---

## 9. Structure / Chorus Questions

Please answer directly:

1. Does the chorus satisfy C's earlier concern that the book needed more than one voice?
2. Are Peter, Ted, Gina, Lee, OMC, and the broader Maya family sufficiently differentiated?
3. Does the chorus feel earned or too rapid?
4. Does “they said okay” land as chorus, sacrament, ordinary mercy, or does it need more grounding?
5. Does “Did I dream the dream, or did the dream dream me?” belong here, or should it move later?
6. Does the uncertainty language help the Autistic reader, or does it become too abstract?

Do not include hidden chain-of-thought or long internal reasoning.

Give conclusions and short rationale.
