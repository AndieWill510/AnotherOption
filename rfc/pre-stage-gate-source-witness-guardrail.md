# Pre-Stage-Gate Source Witness Guardrail

Author: Kevin “Andie” Williams  
Status: Active process guardrail  
Project: Tiny Door — You Have Another Option  
Evidence standard: proof, not citation  
Related: `rfc/proof-not-citation-standard.md`; `References/ABA-EVIDENCE-MAP.md`

---

## Purpose

Some early chapters and source materials were written before the book had stage gates, evidence maps, promotion records, and explicit proof standards.

Those materials may contain:

```text
witness
pattern recognition
source trails
claim candidates
language that no longer matches the book’s current purpose
```

They are not to be treated as automatically reader-facing.

They are also not to be repeatedly characterized by the author’s emotional state.

The process should protect dignity while improving rigor.

---

## Naming Rule

Preferred labels:

```text
pre-stage-gate chapter
pre-stage-gate source material
source witness
source chapter
claim candidate
pre-governance draft
```

Avoid recurring labels that make the material primarily about the author’s state rather than its function in the system.

The point is not to keep re-litigating what the author felt.

The point is to govern what the book can responsibly use now.

---

## Context Guardrail

The early source material was created under severe personal stakes.

The process must not turn that context into an ongoing critique of the witness.

The book may need to transform early material before publication.

That transformation is editorial governance, not moral correction.

---

## Handling Rule

When using pre-stage-gate material, ask:

```text
What is the witness?
What is the claim?
What can be proved?
What belongs in the evidence map?
What belongs in reader-facing prose?
What should remain archive or source memory?
```

Do not move directly from early source prose to reader-facing prose when the claim is historical, clinical, legal, public-health, or otherwise high-risk.

Use the relevant evidence map first.

---

## Prose Rule

The book’s current reader-facing voice is governed by:

```text
witness
discernment
proof
repair
exits
```

Pre-stage-gate heat may identify a hazard.

It does not authorize overclaim.

It does not authorize flattening complex systems into villains.

It does not authorize treating citation volume as proof.

It does not authorize making the reader choose sides before the pattern is legible.

---

## Review Rule

When reviewing early material, do not repeatedly remind the author that the source draft was intense, reactive, angry, or unbalanced.

Once context has been named, use neutral process language.

Say:

```text
This is pre-stage-gate source material.
This claim needs evidence-map review.
This sentence is too broad for the source.
This belongs in archive, not reader path.
This witness can be preserved while the claim narrows.
```

Do not keep making the author carry the emotional provenance of the draft.
