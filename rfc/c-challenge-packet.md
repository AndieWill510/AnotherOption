## 3. What This Project Is

*You Have Another Option* is a book project.

It is not software.

It is a survival-oriented book for Autistic people and those who care about them.

Its hidden engine is suicide prevention.

Its larger purpose is Autistic longevity.

Its method is another option.

Current working description:

> This is a book about building an Autistic life your nervous system can survive long enough to love.

The book is meant to be intimate, practical, fierce, scientifically humble, Autistic-affirming, and reader-safe.

## 6. Current Architecture Claim

The repository currently contains 44 imported chapter files under `Chapters/`.

The current architecture claim is:

> The imported files are source material, not destiny.

The proposed reader-facing book should not remain 44 long chapters.

The proposed structure is:

```text
44 imported source bins
→ 7 parts
→ approximately 12 reader-facing chapters
→ 60–120 short Options
```

The small unit is called an **Option**.

Each Option should be readable in one private pause.

Working rule:

> One private sitting. One survivable idea. One concrete next move.

C should challenge this architecture.

## 9. What C Should Do

C should produce a challenge memo with these sections:

### A. Executive Judgment

Answer briefly:

- Does the 7-part / ~12-chapter / Options model work?
- What is the strongest part of the architecture?
- What is the weakest part?
- What is the most urgent repair?

### B. Reader Safety Challenge

Assess whether the architecture protects readers who may be:

- suicidal or near-crisis;
- dysregulated;
- ashamed;
- overloaded;
- traumatized by ABA, family, school, clinicians, workplaces, or relationships;
- isolated;
- living with limited money, executive function, social support, or safe housing.

Look especially for:

- fatalism;
- shame leakage;
- false simplicity;
- overpromising;
- advice that assumes too much capacity;
- crisis language that needs more care;
- rage that may overwhelm rather than protect the reader.

### C. Scientific Humility Challenge

Identify where the project may need softer or more precise language around:

- Autistic lifespan estimates;
- suicide risk;
- drowning / choking / epilepsy statistics;
- ABA harms;
- trauma physiology;
- cortisol and stress effects;
- oxytocin / dopamine / endorphin claims;
- service animals;
- sex-positive autism;
- intersectional risk claims;
- diagnostic prevalence claims.

C does not need to fact-check everything.

C should flag categories of claims that require careful verification before publication.

### D. Structure Challenge

Assess:

- Is the opening sequence strong enough?
- Does the saxophone metaphor belong at the true beginning?
- Does mortality enter too early, too late, or in the wrong tone?
- Does the book give relief early enough?
- Does Part III risk overpowering the survival path?
- Does Part VI risk tokenization?
- Should `References` and the trauma/cortisol material be back matter rather than chapters?
- Does `Option` work as the unit name?

### E. Missing Pathways

Identify missing or underdeveloped reader pathways, such as:

- newly diagnosed adult;
- parent of a young Autistic child;
- Autistic teen;
- non-speaking or high-support-needs Autistic people;
- Autistic reader in active burnout;
- Autistic reader in unsafe housing;
- Autistic reader with distrust of clinicians;
- Autistic reader harmed by therapy;
- reader outside the United States;
- reader without money or insurance.

### F. Cut / Hold / Move Recommendations

C should suggest what belongs in:

- main reader path;
- back matter;
- reference store;
- workbook / companion guide;
- author note;
- later volume;
- cut or quarantine.

### G. Smallest Next Safe Move

C should recommend the smallest next move that improves the project without destabilizing it.
