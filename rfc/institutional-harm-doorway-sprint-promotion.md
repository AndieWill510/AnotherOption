## 6. What the Sprint Proved

Core arc:

```text
Help for whom?
Behavior is not the whole person.
Normal was never the goal.
```

The sprint establishes:

- help must be evaluated by whose safety and freedom it increases;
- behavior should not be treated as the whole person;
- visible compliance can hide harm;
- support and compliance may share tools but point toward different futures;
- normal-looking is not the same as safe, loved, or free;
- therapy/support must be distinguished from conversion, self-erasure, and system convenience.

---

## 7. Guardrails Preserved

This sprint is promoted under the following guardrails:

```text
no trauma spectacle
no demonizing individual caregivers by default
no false equivalence
no "all therapy is abuse"
clear distinction between support, accommodation, and compliance training
Maslow 1 compression standard from the start
```

Additional guardrails:

- avoid overclaiming historical facts before evidence pass;
- preserve distinction between caregiver exhaustion and institutional harm;
- keep child-safety language protective without sensationalizing harm;
- avoid making the vulnerable reader relive trauma for the sake of rhetoric.

## 8. Evidence / Citation Flags

Evidence review required for:

- ABA history;
- behaviorism claims;
- claims about compliance training and harm;
- claims about conversion therapy relationship / lineage;
- citations and claims from `Chapters/070.md` before historical sprint promotion;
- distinction between current ABA practices, historical ABA practices, and non-ABA support / therapies;
- lived-experience claims vs. clinical claims vs. public-health claims.

No sentence should use poetic force to carry a scientific, clinical, or historical claim that has not yet been supported.
