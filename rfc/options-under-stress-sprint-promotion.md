## 4. Guardrails Preserved

This sprint is promoted under the following guardrails:

```text
no toxic positivity
no "choose happiness"
no shame for despair
no inspirational mug language
no fake certainty
one concrete option per chapter
```

Additional safety constraints:

- do not imply despair is moral failure;
- do not imply attitude alone creates safety;
- do not blame the reader for panic, shutdown, trauma response, or option collapse;
- preserve low-capacity next moves;
- keep hope practical and reachable.

---

## 5. What the Sprint Proved

Core arc:

```text
Positive is not the point.
Keep one door unlocked.
Hope needs handles.
```

The sprint establishes:

- the reader does not need to feel positive to preserve one option;
- under stress, the goal is not cheerfulness but access to the next usable move;
- planning is not pessimism;
- scripts, exits, snacks, quiet places, support people, and rides home are option-preservation tools;
- abstract hope can become another demand if it does not change access;
- hope with handles should reduce load rather than increase obligation.

## 7. Safety Flag Thread — Carry Forward to Evidence Review

Andie explicitly instructed:

```text
Continue the safety flag thread into the evidence review pass for this sprint.
```

The following safety / evidence flags must be carried into the evidence review pass:

### 7.1 Mindset / Survival Claims

Claims that mindset, panic, stress, or despair affect survival, safety, decision quality, or option access require evidence review.

Review for:

- overclaiming;
- reader-blame;
- implied moral responsibility for panic;
- false equivalence between military survival training and Autistic daily life;
- whether the Special Forces / PMA source analogy should remain, soften, or be moved to evidence notes.

### 7.2 Stress Narrowing / Cognitive Access Claims

Claims that stress narrows choices, creative thinking, language access, social options, or help-seeking require evidence review.

Review for:

- clinical accuracy;
- trauma-informed wording;
- autism-specific evidence where available;
- avoiding claims that suggest a reader should simply think better under stress.

### 7.3 Hope / Despair Safety Claims

Claims about hope, despair, hopelessness, practical hope, or keeping one option alive require safety-style review.

Review for:

- suicide-prevention safety;
- avoiding fatalism;
- avoiding shame for readers who currently cannot access hope;
- ensuring crisis-facing language remains practical and non-coercive.

### 7.4 Planning / Option-Preservation Claims

Claims about scripts, exits, snacks, quiet places, support people, and rides home as option-preservation tools require practical accessibility review.

Review for:

- readers without money, housing, transportation, phone access, safe people, or private space;
- readers under surveillance, family control, school control, workplace control, institutional control, or carceral/policing risk;
- not assuming every reader can leave, text, eat, rest, or disclose.

### 7.5 Evidence Register

Before external review, distinguish clearly between:

```text
memoir / lived experience
practical suggestion
clinical claim
public-health claim
research-supported statement
uncertain hypothesis
metaphor
```

No sentence should use poetic force to carry a scientific or clinical claim that has not yet been supported.

## 8. Open Flags

Evidence / citation flags:

- Positive psychology / PMA claims require source review;
- stress and cognitive narrowing claims require source review;
- practical hope / suicide-prevention language requires safety review;
- Special Forces analogy requires sensitivity review and possible demilitarization;
- lifespan / longevity references in the source PMA material remain evidence-review items;
- option-preservation practices should be reviewed for accessibility across disability, poverty, race, gender, housing, and policing contexts.

Editorial flags:

- avoid military cosplay or survivalist overreach;
- avoid turning hope into a command;
- avoid implying positive mindset is a substitute for community, accommodation, medical care, safety, income, housing, or justice;
- preserve Tiny Door scale: one next handle, not a whole life strategy;
- keep the anti-fortune-cookie audit active.
