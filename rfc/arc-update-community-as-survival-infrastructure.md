## 3. Arc Update

Since writing the 2023–2024 source material, Andie has lived the book's thesis.

Andie found community: a large group of neurodivergent friends in Charlottesville, Virginia, who adopted her.

That community saved her life.

Not metaphorically.

As a survival mechanism.

Exactly as the book argues community can function.

From that ground of safety, Andie was strong enough to transition to a woman.

This is not separate from the book.

This is the book's argument with a life attached to it.

---

## 4. What This Changes for the Book

The book now has a lived proof of concept:

```text
community can become survival infrastructure;
stayable life can become real;
safety can create enough ground for becoming;
joy and community are not theory;
reader survival is not only individual technique.
```

This answers an absence identified in challenge review:

- no other person fully present in the book yet;
- no community as felt experience yet;
- joy appearing more as theory than as lived survival;
- Part VII present as architecture but not yet embodied.

The Charlottesville community story can answer these from the inside.

---

## 5. Arc Function

The Charlottesville story may serve as:

- the answer to "what does community as survival infrastructure actually look like?";
- the proof that `stayable` is real and achievable;
- the emotional counterweight before the darkest valley material;
- the living version of Part VII — `the life you can stay for`;
- a bridge between survival, joy, transition, community, and becoming;
- a reminder that the book's thesis is not merely theoretical.
